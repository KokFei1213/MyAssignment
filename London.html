LONDON

London is the capital city of England. It is the most popular city in the United Kingdom, with a metropolitan area of over 13 million inhabitants.

Popular Attractions

  • Big Ben

Big Ben: The Time - Honored Legend of London

Big Ben, now known as the Elizabeth Tower, is an iconic building in London and even the United Kingdom. It is located at the northern end of the Palace of Westminster on the banks of the River Thames in London. This Gothic - style building stands 96 meters tall, with a bell weighing 13.7 tons, and is famous worldwide for its accurate time - keeping and resounding chimes.

Construction of Big Ben began in 1858. Over the long years, it has witnessed numerous historical moments and carries the profound historical and cultural heritage of the UK. During the air raids of World War II, it stood firm, inspiring the people. From 2017 to 2021, Big Ben underwent restoration and has since regained its former glory.

Buckingham Palace: The Palace of British Royal Glory

Buckingham Palace, the main residence and office of the British monarch, is located in the City of Westminster, London. It stands as a symbol of British royal culture, exuding regal dignity and glory.

The palace's exterior is magnificent, with its neoclassical style showcasing solemn grandeur. Inside, the opulent decorations and a vast collection of precious artworks turn it into a treasure trove of art. The famous Changing of the Guard ceremony takes place daily or every other day in the forecourt of the palace, attracting countless tourists.

Encounter the London Eye and Embrace the Most Stunning View of London

Welcome to London! The London Eye, standing on the south bank of the River Thames, soars 135 meters high and is an iconic structure of the city. Since its opening in 1999, it has attracted over 3 million tourists every year.

It features 32 observation capsules. The whole ride takes about 30 minutes. As you take the ride, more than 55 famous sights, such as Big Ben and Buckingham Palace, will come into full view, accompanied by informative commentary. Whether in the daytime or at night, the London Eye offers you a unique viewing experience.

London Cuisine: The Flavors of Britain on Your Taste Buds

London's cuisine is a unique blend of diverse cultures, boasting its own charm.

Signature Dishes

Fish and chips is a must - try classic. The golden and crispy fried fish, paired with French fries, and dipped in a special sauce, offers a mouthful of satisfaction. The Shepherd's Pie is also not to be missed. Tender mutton stewed with vegetables, topped with fluffy mashed potatoes, creates a dish with rich layers and a full, intense flavor.

Street Food

Curry sausage can be found everywhere. The sausage combined with a rich and fragrant curry sauce has a distinctive flavor that's addictive. And there are also hot roasted chestnuts, which are sweet, soft, and glutinous. They are a heartwarming and delicious treat on cold days. Come to London and embark on a wonderful culinary journey!

London: A Melting Pot of Culture and Festivities

Culture

London's cultural scene is incredibly diverse. Its world - renowned museums, like the British Museum, house artifacts from all over the globe, offering a journey through human history. The West End is the heart of theater in London, with long - running musicals and classic plays performed in magnificent theaters. Art galleries such as the Tate Modern showcase both modern and contemporary art, attracting art lovers worldwide. Additionally, the city's architecture is a blend of the old and the new, from the historic Tower of London to the modern Shard skyscraper.

Festivals

The Notting Hill Carnival is one of London's most famous festivals. It takes place annually in August and is a celebration of Caribbean culture, featuring colorful parades, steel - pan music, and delicious street food. Another important event is the New Year's Day Parade, where various marching bands, performers, and floats fill the streets, kicking off the new year with a bang. Christmas in London is also a magical time, with festive markets, ice - skating rinks, and the illumination of Oxford Street.

The Best Time To Visit London

The best time to visit London depends on your preference. Summer (June - August), with 18 - 25°C, is great for outdoor fun like festivals. Spring (March - May), around 11 - 15°C, offers blooming parks and cultural events. Autumn (September - November) has mild weather, around 10 - 20°C, and rich culture. Winter (December - February), 0 - 8°C, is cold but has a festive Christmas.
