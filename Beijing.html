Beijing

Beijing is the capital of China.

It is the political, cultural, and economic center of China.

Popular Attractions

Must - Visit Landmarks

  • The Forbidden City

    • The imperial palace of the Ming and Qing dynasties. Its magnificent architecture and rich collections offer a glimpse into the grandeur and history of the ancient royal court.

  • Tiananmen Square

    • One of the largest city squares in the world. You can watch the solemn flag - raising ceremony here.

    Famous Scenic Spots

  • The Badaling Great Wall

    • An outstanding section of the Great Wall of China. It winds among overlapping mountains, presenting a magnificent and spectacular view.

  • The Summer Palace

    • A large - scale imperial garden. With its lakes, mountains, long corridors and ancient buildings, it is a place of great beauty.

Hidden Gems

  • Shijia Hutong Museum

    • Hidden deep in the hutongs, it showcases the courtyard culture and the life of ordinary people in old Beijing.

  • Xishiku Church

    • A Gothic - style building with unique stained glass and exquisite sculptures.

Cuisine and Local Delicacies

Signature Dishes

Beijing Roast Duck

The crispy skin and tender meat are served with thin pancakes, scallions, cucumbers and sweet bean sauce, providing a rich taste experience.

Instant - Boiled Mutton

Cooked in a copper pot over charcoal fire with a clear soup base. The tender mutton is dipped in a sesame - paste sauce, filling your mouth with fragrance.

Must - Try Street Foods

Douzhi with Jiaquan

Douzhi has a unique taste and is paired with crispy jiaquan, a favorite of old - Beijing residents.

Zha Jiang Mian

flavored fried sauce is served with chewy noodles and various side dishes, with a strong aroma.

Culture and Festivals

Beijing is a city with a rich cultural heritage.

It hosts various traditional festivals throughout the year, such as the Spring Festival and Mid-Autumn Festival.

Spring Festival

It is bustling with traditional performances, delicious snacks and handicrafts.

The Mid - Autumn Festival

It is a time for family reunions and enjoying mooncakes under the full moon.
